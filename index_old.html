Our Mission

Built for the
community, by us

Soft Queer Wealth is a Black queer digital media platform and community resource centering Black LGBTQ+ women, femmes, and queer people across the DMV and beyond.

We create culturally specific storytelling, event discovery, lifestyle content, relationship education, and community resources that help Black queer audiences find connection, visibility, joy, and intentional experiences.

Our vision is a world where Black queer communities have infrastructure — not just content. A living ecosystem where the guide, the data, and the community all talk to each other.

GreenBook introduces a community-rated review layer — a Michelin Star model built on our survey data. Organizers and venues earn ratings based on safety, inclusion, representation, and experience quality as scored by our community.

Venues and organizers that score low get access to our consulting services — actionable guidance on how to better serve Black queer audiences. The data creates accountability; the consulting creates change.

Our Data Integrity
Process

We built this guide through a multi-step public data collection and manual verification process — designed to make Black Pride planning easier, more accurate, and more trustworthy.

How We Work

Five steps to
trustworthy data

1

Public Event Data Collection

Our team begins by collecting publicly available event information from a wide range of sources. Because Black Pride events are spread across many different platforms, our first goal is to centralize information that is otherwise difficult to find in one place.

Instagram Eventbrite Posh Dice Venue Websites Community Calendars Nightlife Listings
2

Manual Data Review

After initial collection, each event is manually reviewed by our team. This pass helps reduce duplicate listings, outdated promotions, and missing event details.

Event Dates + Times Venue Validation Ticket Links Organizer Names Audience Fit Active Status Check
3

Cross-Platform Verification

Whenever possible, event information is compared across multiple public sources. If an event is first found via an Instagram flyer, we look for supporting confirmation through ticketing pages, venue calendars, or public reposts to ensure details align.

4

Crowd-Sourced Event Validation

Some Black Pride events circulate primarily through community sharing or word of mouth. For these, we only include listings after confirming public-facing organizer activity and active promotion. We do not include private, unverified, or rumor-based submissions.

5

Ongoing Human Review

Although our guide uses structured data organization, every event still passes through human review before publication. This project combines public data aggregation with community-informed manual validation — not blind automation. Black Pride weekend information can shift quickly; we always recommend checking final organizer pages before attending.

Data Schema

What we track — and
why it matters

Every event is tied to a real place, time, and vibe. Here's the structured data model behind the DC Black Pride Guide — built to answer not just where to go, but what it will feel like to be there.

📍

Venue + Location

  • Venue Name
  • Address (mapping + validation)
  • Neighborhood (Dupont, U Street…)
🗓

Event Details

  • Event Name
  • Date + Day of Week
  • Start / End Time
  • Price Range
  • Ticket Link
  • Organizer
🎉

Event Type

  • Day Party
  • Club
  • Ball
  • Outdoor
  • Pool
✨

Vibe Tags

  • Ass Shaking
  • Flirt
  • Chill
  • Groove
  • Creative
♿

Accessibility

  • Wheelchair Entry (yes/partial/no/unknown)
  • Elevator Access
  • Multi-Level Venue
  • Accessible Restroom
  • Gender Neutral Restrooms
  • Crowd Density (low/medium/high)
  • Accessibility Score scored
🧠

Derived Fields

  • Indoor vs Outdoor derived
  • Mobility Friendliness derived
  • Energy Level (low/medium/high) derived
  • Human Notes (contextual)

"Most event lists tell you where to go and what time. We wanted to tell you what it's going to feel like — and whether your body can exist there comfortably."

This isn't just a dataset. It's structured + experiential community knowledge. Some things are marked unknown or partial — that's intentional. We'd rather be uncertain than wrong where it matters.

Research Methods

Community Intelligence
Survey Framework

A proposed 5-question post-event survey designed to collect the community intelligence that powers GreenBook's ratings, insights, and consulting layer.

🎓

These survey questions are proposed for review with our Research Advisor, Theo Beltran, Ph.D. — developed collaboratively to ensure methodological integrity, ethical collection practices, and community-centered design.

Survey v1 · 5 Questions

What we ask — and
why we ask it

1
Overall Experience · Anchor Metric
"How was your experience at this event?"
  • 🔥 Loved it
  • 😊 It was good
  • 😐 It was okay
  • 😕 Not for me

What this gives us

  • Top-line performance score per event
  • Easy benchmark to compare across events over time
  • Foundation for GreenBook's overall event rating
2
Vibe Alignment · Our Differentiator
"Did the vibe match what you expected?"
  • ✅ Yes, exactly what I was looking for
  • 〰️ Mostly, but a little off
  • 🤔 Not really
  • ❌ Not at all

What this gives us

  • Expectation vs. reality gap analysis per event
  • Helps organizers fix branding and messaging
  • Helps us improve vibe tagging and filtering in GreenBook
3
Community + Safety · Critical for Our Brand
"How comfortable did you feel in the space?"
  • 💚 Very comfortable / safe
  • 🟡 Mostly comfortable
  • 🟠 Somewhat uncomfortable
  • 🔴 Not comfortable

What this gives us

  • Queer safety and inclusion scoring per venue
  • Crowd dynamics and who is actually showing up
  • Feeds directly into GreenBook's community safety rating
  • Accountability data for venues that score consistently low
4
What Stood Out · Qualitative Gold
"What stood out most about this event?" (select all that apply)
  • 🎵 Music / DJ
  • 👯‍♀️ Crowd / people
  • 🏢 Venue / space
  • 🚪 Organization (lines, entry, flow)
  • ✊🏾 Representation (Black queer women/femmes present)
  • 💸 Price / value
  • ✏️ Other: ______

What this gives us

  • What actually drives satisfaction in our community
  • What organizers should double down on
  • Qualitative signals that numbers alone can't capture
5
Return Intent · Retention Signal
"Would you attend an event by this organizer again?"
  • 💜 Definitely — already looking for the next one
  • 👍 Probably yes
  • 🤷‍♀️ Maybe, depends on the vibe
  • 👎 Probably not

What this gives us

  • Organizer loyalty score — key metric for consulting tier
  • Paired with Q1 to separate one-time vs. recurring community trust
  • Feeds the Michelin Star rating system for organizer accountability

Survey v1 — In Review with Theo Beltran, Ph.D.

This is the proposed first version of our community intelligence survey, designed for post-event collection. Questions will be refined in collaboration with our Research Advisor to ensure methodological integrity, reduce bias, and maximize actionable insight for both community members and the organizers and venues we partner with.
