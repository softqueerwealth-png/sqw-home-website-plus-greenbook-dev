Our Mission

Built for the
community, by us

Soft Queer Wealth is a Black queer digital media platform and community resource centering Black LGBTQ+ women, femmes, and queer people across the DMV and beyond.

We create culturally specific storytelling, event discovery, lifestyle content, relationship education, and community resources that help Black queer audiences find connection, visibility, joy, and intentional experiences.

Our vision: a world where Black queer communities have infrastructure — not just content. A living ecosystem where the guide, the data, and the community all talk to each other.

Fall · Full Ecosystem

Community Vibe Intelligence + Consulting

GreenBook introduces a community-powered insight layer — not a public shaming tool, but a dignified feedback system built on consent and care. After events, community members can share their honest experience via survey: what the vibe actually felt like, how safe they felt, what stood out.

This insight belongs to two people: the organizer (who gets direct, private feedback on what their community actually experienced — not what they assumed) and the community (who can see aggregated, anonymized vibe data to know what to expect before they show up). Organizers consent before any of their data surfaces publicly.

For organizers who want to go further: we offer consulting — not judgment. If the feedback shows a gap, we help bridge it. That might mean connecting them with an organizer who's excelled in that area, or sitting down together to brainstorm tangible next steps. The goal is always situational awareness first, then execution. You can't improve what you don't know.

Transparency

Our Data Integrity
Process

We built this guide through a multi-step public data collection and manual verification process designed to make Black Pride planning easier, more accurate, and more trustworthy.

How We Work

Five steps to
trustworthy data

1

Public Event Data Collection

Our team begins by collecting publicly available event information from a wide range of sources. Because Black Pride events are spread across many different platforms, our first goal is to centralize information that is otherwise difficult to find in one place.

InstagramEventbritePoshDiceVenue WebsitesCommunity CalendarsNightlife Listings
2

Manual Data Review

After initial collection, each event is manually reviewed by our team — validating dates, times, venues, ticket links, organizer names, audience fit, and whether the event is still publicly active. This pass helps reduce duplicate listings, outdated promotions, and missing details.

3

Cross-Platform Verification

Whenever possible, event information is compared across multiple public sources. If an event is first found via an Instagram flyer, we look for supporting confirmation through ticketing pages, venue calendars, or public reposts to ensure details align and reduce inaccurate listings.

4

Crowd-Sourced Event Validation

Some Black Pride events circulate primarily through community sharing or word of mouth. For these, we only include listings after confirming public-facing organizer activity and active promotion. We do not include private, unverified, or rumor-based submissions.

5

Ongoing Human Review

Every event still passes through human review before publication. This combines public data aggregation with community-informed manual validation — not blind automation. Black Pride weekend information can shift quickly; we always recommend checking final organizer pages before attending.

6

Consent, Anonymization & Community Surveys

As GreenBook grows into community intelligence collection, every participant completes a consent form before their data is included. All survey responses are aggregated and anonymized — we never publish individual responses. Participants may optionally share their email to stay updated or get more involved, but this is never required.

Insight surfaced publicly is always aggregated, never individual, and organizer-level data is only shared with their explicit consent. We hold data quality and integrity as a core value — not an afterthought.

Consent FormsAnonymized DataAggregated InsightsOptional Email
7

Focus Groups & Qualitative Research

Beyond surveys, we plan to run focus groups — smaller, deeper conversations with community members about their experiences at curated events and with local businesses. These qualitative interviews surface the texture and nuance that survey data alone can't capture.

Focus groups are voluntary, compensated where possible, and always conducted with the same consent and anonymization standards as our survey research. This qualitative layer strengthens both our community insights and our consulting offering to organizers.

Research Methods

Community Intelligence
Survey Framework

A proposed 5-question post-event survey designed to collect the community intelligence that powers GreenBook's ratings, insights, and consulting layer.

🎓

These survey questions are proposed for review with our Research Advisor, Theo Beltran, Ph.D. — developed collaboratively to ensure methodological integrity, ethical collection practices, and community-centered design.

Survey v1 · 5 Questions

What we ask —
and why we ask it

1
Overall Experience · Anchor Metric
"How was your experience at this event?"
  • 🔥 Loved it
  • 😊 It was good
  • 😐 It was okay
  • 😕 Not for me

What this gives us

  • Top-line performance score per event
  • Easy benchmark to compare across events over time
  • Foundation for GreenBook's overall event rating
2
Vibe Alignment · Our Differentiator
"Did the vibe match what you expected?"
  • ✅ Yes, exactly what I was looking for
  • 〰️ Mostly, but a little off
  • 🤔 Not really
  • ❌ Not at all

What this gives us

  • Expectation vs. reality gap per event
  • Helps organizers fix branding and messaging
  • Improves our vibe tagging and filtering in GreenBook
3
Community + Safety · Critical for Our Brand
"How comfortable did you feel in the space?"
  • 💚 Very comfortable / safe
  • 🟡 Mostly comfortable
  • 🟠 Somewhat uncomfortable
  • 🔴 Not comfortable

What this gives us

  • Queer safety and inclusion scoring per venue
  • Crowd dynamics and who is actually showing up
  • Feeds into GreenBook's community safety rating
  • Accountability data for venues that score consistently low
4
What Stood Out · Qualitative Gold
"What stood out most about this event?" (select all that apply)
  • 🎵 Music / DJ
  • 👯‍♀️ Crowd / people
  • 🏢 Venue / space
  • 🚪 Organization (lines, entry, flow)
  • ✊🏾 Representation (Black queer women/femmes)
  • 💸 Price / value
  • ✏️ Other: ______

What this gives us

  • What actually drives satisfaction in our community
  • What organizers should double down on
  • Qualitative signals that numbers alone can't capture
5
Return Intent · Retention Signal
"Would you attend an event by this organizer again?"
  • 💜 Definitely — already looking for the next one
  • 👍 Probably yes
  • 🤷‍♀️ Maybe, depends on the vibe
  • 👎 Probably not

What this gives us

  • Organizer loyalty score — a key signal for our consulting layer
  • Paired with Q1 to separate one-time vs. recurring community trust
  • Helps us understand which organizers are building lasting relationships with our community

Survey v1 — In Review with Theo Beltran, Ph.D.
